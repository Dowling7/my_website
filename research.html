Particle Identification for Dark Photon Detection

A dark sector upgrade to SpinQuest at the 120 GeV Fermilab Main Injector

Accelerator-based searches for dark matter and dark sector particles are a uniquely compelling way to both create and detect dark matter in the laboratory. The DarkQuest experiment is a proposed upgrade to the current SpinQuest experiment at Fermilab, where dark photons could be created as 120 GeV protons interact with a large block of iron. The proposed upgrade includes the addition of an electromagnetic calorimeter to the existing drift-chamber tracking spectrometer. While SpinQuest focuses exclusively on di-muon production and detection, the DarkQuest experiment hopes to be sensitive to additional particle types, such as electrons, photons, and pions.

My work focuses on developing a particle ID algorithm based on the proposed DarkQuest configuration. This algorithm aims to increase the fiducial area for particle discrimination, extending sensitivity to dark photons with different energies and lifetimes. In the poster above, we describe the first version of particle discriminators that incorporate multiple detector components using Neural Networks.
